Vietnamese Pickled Carrots and Daikon (Dưa Chua Cà Rốt và Củ Cải)

Vietnam · Condiment

Staple Vietnamese pickles for bánh mì, noodle bowls, and grilled meats. Key is keeping veg submerged.

Serves: 4 cups (approx. 960ml) · Prep: 15 minutes · Wait: 2 hours · Cook: 0 minutes

Ingredients

For the Pickled Vegetables

  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and cut into 3mm batons (Note 1)
  • 1/2 large daikon (white radish), peeled and cut into 3mm batons (Note 1)
  • 375ml boiling water
  • 100g white sugar
  • 20g cooking or kosher salt
  • 180ml rice wine vinegar (Note 2)

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, pour the boiling water over the sugar and salt. Stir until dissolved.
  2. Add the vinegar and stir to combine.
  3. Add carrot and daikon, submerging well; press down if needed.
  4. Let sit 2 hours to soften slightly.
  5. Drain the portion you need to serve now, or transfer to a jar ensuring all veg are submerged.
  6. Refrigerate; keeps up to 2 months.

Notes

  • Cut size: 3mm batons help even pickling and texture.
  • Vinegar: Rice wine vinegar is best; apple cider vinegar is a workable substitute but tangier.
  • Storage tip: Keep vegetables submerged; glass jars preferred.

Storage & Reheating

  • Store submerged in liquid in a glass container in the fridge, up to 2 months.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with bánh mì, noodle bowls, or grilled meats.
