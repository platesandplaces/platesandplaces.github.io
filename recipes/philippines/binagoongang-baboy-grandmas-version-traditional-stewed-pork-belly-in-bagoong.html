Binagoongang Baboy – Grandma’s Version (Traditional Stewed Pork Belly in Bagoong)

Philippines · Main

Comforting stewed pork belly simmered gently with sautéed shrimp paste and vinegar.

Serves: 3–4 · Prep: 10 minutes · Wait: 0 minutes · Cook: 1 hour

Ingredients

Ingredients

  • 600g pork belly, skin on, chunks
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 5 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp sautéed shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tsp sugar (optional)
  • 1 long green chilli (optional)
  • 1 cup water
  • Steamed rice, to serve

Instructions

  1. Render some pork fat and brown lightly; set fat aside leaving 1–2 tbsp.
  2. Sauté garlic and onion; add bagoong 2–3 min.
  3. Add vinegar; boil 1 min without stirring. Add sugar if using and water (and chilli if using).
  4. Cover and simmer low 45–60 min until tender and sauce thick. Serve with rice.

Notes

  • Boiling vinegar briefly removes sharpness.
  • Keep heat low for tenderness and flavour melding.

Storage & Reheating

  • Fridge 4 days; freeze up to 1 month.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with eggplant or okra; great with sour soup like sinigang.
