Sinigang na Baboy (Filipino Sour Pork Soup)

Philippines · Soup

Tangy, savoury pork soup with tamarind — balanced, comforting, and versatile.

Serves: 4–6 · Prep: 20 minutes · Wait: 15 minutes (tamarind soak) · Cook: 45 minutes

Ingredients

For the soup

  • 60 ml vegetable oil
  • 500 g pork ribs
  • 500 g pork belly, 4 cm pieces
  • 1 large onion, wedges
  • 3 tomatoes, wedges
  • 1 small red capsicum, wedges
  • 1.5 L water
  • 4 cm horseradish, peeled, thirds
  • 1 Japanese eggplant, sliced on diagonal
  • 200 g snake/green beans
  • 2 tsp salt; black pepper

For the souring agent

  • 100 g tamarind pulp
  • 250 ml boiling water

To serve

  • Sliced spring onions; steamed rice; fish sauce; kalamansi or lemon wedges

Instructions

  1. Brown ribs and belly; set aside.
  2. Sauté onion, tomatoes, capsicum; return pork and water. Simmer 15 min.
  3. Soak tamarind 15 min; strain and add liquid to pot.
  4. Add horseradish and eggplant; cook 5 min. Add beans 3 min.
  5. Season; adjust sourness with fish sauce or extra tamarind. Serve with rice.

Notes

  • Bone‑in cuts add flavour. Tamarind concentrate can substitute (2–3 tbsp).

Storage & Reheating

  • Fridge 3 days; reheat gently.

Serving Suggestions

  • Excellent next day; thin with water if broth thickens.
