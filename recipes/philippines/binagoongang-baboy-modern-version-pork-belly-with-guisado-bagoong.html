Binagoongang Baboy – Modern Version (Pork Belly with Guisado Bagoong)

Philippines · Main

Crispy double‑cooked pork belly tossed in rich tomato‑bagoong sauce with fresh chillies.

Serves: 3–4 · Prep: 20 minutes · Wait: 0 minutes · Cook: 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients

For the pork

  • 600g boneless pork belly, skin on
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 6 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp fine salt
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • Vegetable oil, for deep frying
  • 1 handful chilli leaves (optional)
  • Steamed rice, to serve

For the guisado bagoong

  • 250g cherry tomatoes
  • 60ml vegetable oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp sautéed shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • 1 long green chilli
  • 2 red bird’s‑eye chillies, sliced

Instructions

  1. Par‑cook pork in seasoned water with aromatics ~1h; cool and cut.
  2. Deep‑fry pork at 180°C ~3 min until crisp; drain.
  3. Fry chilli leaves (optional) 20 sec; drain.
  4. Cook tomatoes, onion, garlic until caramelised; add bagoong 2 min.
  5. Add reserved broth, pork, chillies; simmer 3–4 min. Serve with rice.

Notes

  • Use ripe tomatoes for depth.
  • If using raw bagoong, cook in oil first to mellow.

Storage & Reheating

  • Fridge 3 days; avoid freezing to preserve texture.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with jasmine rice; add grilled eggplant or green mango salad.
