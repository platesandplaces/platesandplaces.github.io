Pork Stir‑Fry (Jeyuk Bokkeum - 제육볶음)

Korea · Main

Spicy‑savory pork stir‑fry with gochujang, pear, garlic; classic Korean favourite.

Serves: 4 · Prep: 15 minutes · Wait: At least 2 hours (marinating) · Cook: 20 minutes

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 500g pork shoulder or belly, thinly sliced
  • 1 zucchini, thin half‑moons
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 2 green chillies, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 100g cabbage or enoki mushrooms (optional)

Marinade

  • 1 small Korean/nashi pear, grated (~1/2 cup)
  • 1/2 small onion, grated (~1/4 cup)
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic (3–4 cloves)
  • 1 tsp minced ginger
  • 3 tbsp gochujang
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce (Korean soup soy or light)
  • 1 tbsp doenjang (optional)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru (optional, to taste)
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp mirin (or splash rice wine/sake)

To Serve

  • Steamed rice, perilla leaves, kimchi, banchan; sesame seeds, sliced green onions

Instructions

  1. Grate pear and onion finely; mix marinade with aromatics and seasonings.
  2. Coat pork and marinate 2h to overnight.
  3. Stir‑fry pork in batches 3–4 minutes until browned. Set aside.
  4. Stir‑fry vegetables 2 minutes; return pork and combine 2–3 minutes. Garnish and serve.

Notes

  • Use glass/ceramic container; gochujang can stain plastic.
  • Go for well‑marbled shoulder/belly; ask butcher for thin slicing.

Storage & Reheating

  • Fridge 3 days; reheat in pan; add splash water if sauce thickens.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with soy braised potatoes, marinated tofu, pickled perilla leaves, kongnamul, oi muchim.
