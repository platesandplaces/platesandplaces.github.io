<div class="recipe-meta">
  <strong>Yield:</strong> ~1 litre<br>
  <strong>Preparation Time:</strong> 5 minutes<br>
  <strong>Cooking Time:</strong> 10 minutes<br>
  <strong>Wait Time:</strong> 30 minutes (optional kombu soak)<br>
</div>

<h4>Ingredients</h4>
<ul>
<li>1 litre cold water</li>
<li>1 piece kombu (~10 cm)</li>
<li>20–30 g katsuobushi (bonito flakes)</li>
</ul>

<h4>Instructions</h4>
<ol>
<li>(Optional) Soak the kombu in the cold water for 30 minutes for deeper flavour.</li>
<li>Heat the water and kombu together over medium heat. Remove the kombu just before boiling (when small bubbles form around the edges) — boiling can make it slimy or bitter.</li>
<li>Add the bonito flakes and immediately turn off the heat.</li>
<li>Let steep 2–3 minutes (the flakes will begin to sink), then strain through a fine sieve or cheesecloth.</li>
</ol>

<h4>Notes</h4>
<ul>
<li><em>Uses:</em> miso soup, oyakodon, nimono (simmered dishes), miso‑glazed eggplant, and light broths.</li>
<li>Kombu can be reused once with fresh water (plus the spent flakes) for a lighter second stock, <em>niban dashi</em>.</li>
</ul>

<h4>Storage & Reheating</h4>
<ul>
<li>Refrigerate 3–5 days.</li>
<li>Freeze in ice‑cube trays for longer storage.</li>
</ul>
